An high school student CTF ?!

A group of student, in which I'm involved, are creating a CTF.

Posted by Akasha on November 03, 2024

Our CTF competition will feature a variety of categories, each designed to test different aspects of cybersecurity knowledge and skills. By participating, students will not only learn about hacking and security, but also gain valuable experience that could prove useful in many future career paths. Here’s a quick breakdown of the categories:

  • Pwn (Exploitation): This category involves exploiting vulnerable programs or systems. Participants will be required to identify security flaws and craft exploits to take control of programs or servers.
  • Programming: In this section, participants will tackle challenges that test their coding and problem-solving abilities. Tasks may involve writing scripts, debugging code, or solving algorithmic challenges.
  • OSINT (Open Source Intelligence): OSINT challenges focus on collecting publicly available data from the internet. Students will learn how to gather, analyze, and interpret information from sources like social media, websites, and databases.
  • Network: In this category, participants will dive into network security. Challenges may include analyzing network traffic, detecting vulnerabilities, or securing communication protocols.
  • Forensics: Forensics challenges are all about investigating digital evidence. Participants will work with logs, file systems, and other traces to uncover what happened in a simulated cyber incident.

These categories are designed to offer a broad spectrum of challenges, ensuring that there’s something for everyone—whether you’re a beginner or someone with more advanced skills. We aim to create a welcoming environment for all students, regardless of their prior experience with cybersecurity. Whether you're interested in cracking code, finding vulnerabilities, or digging through digital clues, there's a category for you to explore and learn!

We look forward to seeing our students' creativity, teamwork, and critical thinking come to life throughout the competition. So, get ready to join the fun, learn some new skills, and perhaps discover a passion for cybersecurity!

What's More in Our CTF?

So, what makes our CTF different from the typical ones you may have seen? We’re taking things up a notch by making this a student vs. teacher showdown! Yes, you read that right—teachers will be competing alongside students in this thrilling event. While the challenges will be individual, the competition will be fierce, as we’ll clearly distinguish between the students and teachers. It’s going to be an all-out battle—who’s got the skills to outsmart the other side?

To make it all happen, we’re using CTFd, an open-source platform designed specifically for running Capture the Flag competitions. It provides a seamless way to organize, score, and track progress, allowing us to focus on the challenges and make the experience even more exciting for everyone involved.

With this unique twist, we hope to encourage collaboration, teamwork, and a bit of friendly rivalry, while also providing an opportunity for everyone to learn and improve their cybersecurity skills in a fun and engaging way. Whether you're a student or a teacher, everyone will be immersed in the same challenges, testing their problem-solving and technical skills to the limit!

How Does the Points System Work?

Now, let's talk about how the points system works. Every challenge starts with a maximum of 500 points, but as time passes and as more people solve the challenge, the point value decreases in a logarithmic fashion. This means the less people solved a challenge, the more points you earn. However, the points will never drop below 100 points, even for the most difficult challenges. So, no matter how tough a challenge gets, there’s always a chance to earn meaningful points.

The key takeaway is: don't focus on just one challenge! While the high-point challenges may seem appealing, the best strategy is to tackle a variety of challenges across all categories. This will not only give you more opportunities to score points, but it will also help you improve your skills in different areas of cybersecurity.

In the end, the person who balances speed, problem-solving, and versatility across all challenges will be the one who comes out on top—whether student or teacher!
